## THE BIG CYCLE — HIS OWN WORDS

**Source:** Fortune, October 2025 / Fortune, March 2026

> *"What I found is that all monetary orders, political orders, and geopolitical orders rise, evolve, and collapse in a repeating pattern I call 'the Big Cycle' — typically lasting about 75 years, give or take about 30."*

> *"I believe that the times ahead will be radically different from what most people have gotten used to — that they will be more like the tumultuous pre-1945 era than what we have experienced since the end of World War II."*

> *"Most people are shocked by what's unfolding in the world right now. I'm not. I've seen this movie before."*

> *"The reason I anticipated the 2008 financial crisis is that I studied the 1930s. The same thing happens over and over again; it's like watching a movie for me."*

**Source:** Acquirer's Multiple (citing Interesting Times with Ross Douthat), May 2026

> *"There is such a thing as a big cycle, and the big cycle starts when there are new orders. There are three types of orders. There's a monetary order, a domestic political order, and an international world order."*

> *"These things are following those kinds of patterns."*

> *"I think we know that we are in increasingly disorderly times."*

> *"My general reaction is that we are entering a period of greater disorder."*

## THE DEBT/MONETARY CRISIS — HIS OWN WORDS

**Source:** Fortune, January 2026 (Davos)

> *"Do you print money or do you let a debt crisis happen?"*

> *"What always scares me is the lack of realism [among leaders]. Will law prevail? Everyone is having to deal with that question."*

**Source:** Fortune, January 2026 (David Rubenstein Show)

> *"My grandchildren and great-grandchildren not yet born are going to be paying off this debt in devalued dollars."*

> *"It's always done when countries essentially go broke. They print money, devalue the currency, and create an artificially low interest rate, so that the person who's holding the bonds is receiving an artificially low interest rate."*

**The mechanism he names — verbatim:**

**Source:** Yahoo Finance / CNBC, December 2025

> *"A debt death spiral is that part of the cycle when the debtor needs to borrow money in order to pay debt service, and it accelerates."*

**The spending gap he cites — his own numbers:**

**Source:** Fortune, May 2026 / NewKerala citing same interview

> *"[The US] spends about $7 trillion annually while generating only $5 trillion in revenue."*

**Note from gate:** The Peter G. Peterson Foundation and CBO figures confirm this gap is real. CBO projects $1.039 trillion in interest payments in 2026 alone — approximately $19-20 billion per week. Dalio's framing understated the interest figure; the underlying gap he describes is sourced and confirmed.

**The Hemingway parallel — directly attributed:**

**Source:** Fortune, January 2026

> Dalio warned debt crises typically develop *"slowly until it happens all at once"* — paraphrasing Hemingway's observation about how bankruptcy happens.

**The monetary breakdown framing:**

**Source:** Fortune, January 2026 (Davos)

> *"The breakdown of the monetary order."*

**Source:** Fortune, March 2026

> *"When this happened at the same time there were large amounts of debt assets (bonds) and debt liabilities (debt) outstanding, as well as large budget deficits that required larger debt asset sales (i.e., bond sales) than there was demand for, the resulting supply/demand imbalance led the value of the debt and/or currency to fall."*

## THE AI ASSESSMENT — HIS OWN WORDS

**Source:** Acquirer's Multiple (Prof G Markets), May 2026

> *"I think a lot of people make the mistake of thinking that because a technology is going to be a revolutionary technology… they buy stocks of that sort without looking really at the price."*

**Source:** Fortune, May 2026

> Dalio sees AI as *"both a potential lifeline — a productivity surge powerful enough to outpace debt accumulation — and a destabilizing threat capable of displacing millions of workers and being weaponized by rival states."*

> *"The days of people making decisions in their own heads are ending."*

**Source:** NewKerala, June 2026

> *"The US and China are in a technology competition. The others are not really in the game. The winner of the technology war will win all wars, including economic war and geopolitical war."*

**Gate note on AI divergence:** Dalio sees AI as both potential remedy and destabilizing threat — a more balanced view than either "accelerant of collapse" (his critics' framing) or "distraction/zombie expansion" (the ensemble's Vector 7 framing). The ensemble and Dalio genuinely disagree on AI's mechanism: Dalio focuses on AI's labor-displacement and geopolitical-weaponization risks; the ensemble tracks AI's negative cash flow and capital misallocation. Both may be simultaneously correct about different aspects of the same phenomenon.
