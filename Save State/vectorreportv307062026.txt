## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Current system state shows **asymmetric stress distribution** with concentrated pressure in energy/geopolitical and agricultural vectors, offset by active adaptation mechanisms in shipping routes, crop substitution, and monetary policy flexibility. The Hormuz crisis represents the primary amplifier, transmitting through fertilizer markets to agriculture and through oil prices to consumer/inflation dynamics. Credit conditions remain stable despite consumer-level stress. Technology vector shows bifurcation: capex surge alongside labor displacement.

**Who benefits?** Livestock operators with manure surplus; specialty crop farmers less fertilizer-dependent; precision ag technology providers.

**Data that would prove concern wrong:** Fertilizer prices declining to pre-crisis levels; 2026 yield forecasts exceeding trend; planted acreage reports showing no corn-to-soybean shift.
