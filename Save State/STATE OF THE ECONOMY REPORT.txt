## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. and global economy are operating under **multi-vector stress with containment-dependent stability** as of July 6, 2026. No major sector shows genuine recovery (+2), while three vectors remain in active deterioration (-2) and four exist in temporary containment (-1) via emergency measures. The system is functional but fragile, with stress transmission occurring through physical supply chains (energy→agriculture→food) and financial channels (credit→consumer→municipal).

**Core Assessment:** The economy is not in collapse, but it is **not resilient**. Stability depends on continued emergency intervention (DOE grid authority, DFC insurance backstop, Fed pause) and favorable resolution of approaching hard deadlines (July 9 Hormuz negotiations, July 10 USDA projections, August 17 Hormuz MOU expiration).

### 2. AGRICULTURE: ENTERING CRITICAL PHASE

**Status:** Active deterioration (-2) with containment buffers

| Metric | Reading | Assessment |
|--------|---------|------------|

| National abandonment | 25.2% (8.16M acres) | 4th highest since 1986 |

### 4. CONSUMER ECONOMY: BIFURCATED STRESS

**Status:** K-shaped divergence — prime stable, subprime deteriorating

| Segment | Status | Evidence |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Mass retailers (Walmart/Costco) | Stable | 7.3% revenue growth, 6.6% comparable sales |
| Discretionary (gaming, frozen meals) | Deteriorating | Microsoft gaming -33% hardware, Conagra distress |
| FHA mortgages | **Active deterioration** | 11.88% delinquency (+36 bps QoQ), 6-year high foreclosures |
| Conventional mortgages | Stable | 2.75% delinquency, rate decline supportive |
| Credit cards | Elevated | 13.1% delinquency (16-year high), auto loans deteriorating |

**Key Insight:** Consumer stress is **segmented, not universal**. Lower-income/FHA borrowers face distress while prime-tier remains stable. Food inflation (33.3% since 2020) and energy costs compress purchasing power at the margin, triggering trade-down behavior (frozen meals to fresh, gaming hardware to services).

**Critical Catalyst:** July 15 Conagra earnings — potential dividend cut would signal absorber (consumer staples) shrinking.

**Status:** Emergency lifted, structural vulnerability remains

| Metric | Reading | Assessment |
|--------|---------|------------|
| PJM emergency orders | Lifted July 3 | Successful containment |
| Peak demand | 166,147 MW (record) | Physical ceiling tested |
| Spot pricing | $2,500+/MWh during stress | 83x normal, extreme volatility |

**Key Insight:** The grid **did not fail** — emergency protocols (load curtailment, backup generators, pollution waivers) prevented collapse. But the system operated at physical ceiling with human casualties. The "success" was containment, not resilience.

**Structural Conflict:** AI datacenter growth (Vector 7) directly competes with residential/commercial load for constrained grid capacity. This is now a **binding physical constraint**, not a planning issue.

## CONCLUSION

The economy as of July 6, 2026, is **functional but not resilient**. Multiple verified stressors (energy, agriculture, grid, credit, municipal) are being contained via emergency measures, not resolved via structural adaptation. Stability depends on favorable resolution of approaching hard deadlines (Hormuz negotiations, USDA projections, MOU expiration).

**The central question is not whether stress exists** — it is verified across 13 vectors. **The question is whether emergency containment holds long enough for structural resilience to develop**, or whether approaching deadlines trigger synchronization before mitigation deploys.

The bull case (S&P +14.9% Q2, earnings +23%) depends entirely on AI and energy — the two sectors most exposed to the physical constraints the ensemble has identified. This is **concentration risk masquerading as strength**.

**Assessment:** Containment-dependent stability. Watch July 9-15 cluster for directional resolution.
