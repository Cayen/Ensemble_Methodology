EVIDENCE HIERARCHY

RUMOR
Definition:
Information with no verifiable chain of attribution.

Characteristics:
• Anonymous social media posts
• Unnamed "sources say"
• Screenshots without provenance
• Viral claims

Action:
Do not operationalize.
May enter WATCHLIST only if potential impact is extremely high.

↓

UNCORROBORATED
Definition:
A claim from one identifiable source that has not yet been independently corroborated.

Characteristics:
• Local newspaper
• Single Reuters exclusive
• One government official
• One eyewitness
• Company press release

Action:
Treat as provisional.
Flag for verification.

↓

SOURCED
Definition:
The claim has a traceable, attributable source and can be accurately cited.

Characteristics:
• Source identity known
• Quote verified
• Numbers match original publication
• Attribution preserved

Important:
"SOURCED" does NOT imply true.
It means the claim is accurately attributed.

Action:
Eligible for verification.

This distinction is subtle but important. Reuters can accurately report "The White House says X." That statement is sourced even if X later proves false.

↓

VERIFIED
Definition:
Multiple independent sources or primary evidence confirm the underlying fact.

Characteristics:
• Independent corroboration
• Official documents
• Multiple reputable outlets
• Primary data available

Action:
May be treated as factual.
Eligible for integration.

↓

INTEGRATED
Definition:
The verified fact has been evaluated for relevance and incorporated into the analytical framework.

Characteristics:
• Cross-vector effects identified
• Mechanisms documented
• Confidence assigned
• Dependencies mapped

Action:
Included in reports and synthesis.

↓

OPERATIONAL
Definition:
The integrated evidence is actively influencing current analytical assumptions and monitoring priorities.

Characteristics:
• Changes active vector assessments
• Alters WATCHLIST priorities
• Drives board briefings
• Included in ongoing operational decisions

Action:
Becomes part of the live operational model until superseded or falsified.
